THE SYSTEM

Inventory System

The Challenge

The narrative required players to carry multiple puzzle items, but standard list-based inventories felt clinical. They often pulled the player out of the game world into a spreadsheet, breaking the atmospheric immersion we worked hard to build.

The Solution

I designed a grid-based drag-and-drop system. Players can physically grab items from a customizable hotbar and drag them directly into the scene to solve puzzles. This makes the interface feel like a tangible part of the world rather than an overlay.

The Result

THE BREAK

Boxing Minigame

The Challenge

The game is a slow-paced psychological narrative. We realized players needed a way to break the tension and release energy without leaving the story context. Constant reading was becoming fatigue-inducing.

The Solution

I prototyped a rhythm-based boxing minigame. By using simple button-mashing inputs that scale in difficulty, we created a spike in engagement. It serves as a narrative device for the character's frustration while giving the player a mechanical break.

The Result

THE WORLD

Interactive World

The Challenge

Purely visual environments often feel static. We needed the world itself to act as a narrative delivery device without forcing heavy text dumps or cutscenes on the player at every turn.

The Solution

I implemented an interaction system where every object has "discovery" states. Hovering reveals subtle cues, and clicking triggers character thoughts specific to that object's narrative weight, ensuring exploration is always rewarded with story context.
